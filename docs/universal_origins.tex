\section{Introduction}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Standard Big Bang cosmology is extraordinarily successful.
It predicts the cosmic microwave background (CMB) temperature, the primordial
element abundances from Big Bang nucleosynthesis (BBN), and the large-scale
structure of the universe to sub-percent precision~\cite{Planck2020,PDG2022}.
Yet it provides no answer to the most basic question: \emph{why did the
universe begin with those specific initial conditions?}
The flatness of space ($\Omega = 1$), the radiation-dominated equation of
state ($w = \tfrac{1}{3}$), the homogeneity of the CMB to one part in
$10^5$ across causally disconnected regions — all of these are inputs to
the standard model, not outputs.

Inflationary cosmology partially addresses this by postulating an epoch of
accelerated expansion driven by a scalar inflaton field, stretching a
causally connected seed to super-horizon scales~\cite{Guth1981,Linde1982}.
However, inflation requires the existence of an inflaton field not present
in the Standard Model (SM), introduces new fine-tuning problems, and does
not address what preceded the inflationary epoch.

The Theory of Universal Origins (TUO) proposes a different approach.
Rather than adding new fields or parameters, it identifies a single
structural principle — the \emph{Global Zero-Sum Constraint} (Axiom~II) —
from which the observed initial conditions follow as mathematical
consequences.
TUO is not a replacement for the Big Bang but its physical precondition:
a framework that operates in the regime $t < \tPl$, hands off exact initial
conditions at $t = \tPl$, and then reduces to standard Big Bang cosmology
for all $t > \tPl$.

\section{Axioms}\label{sec:axioms}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\begin{axiom}[Flat Background]\label{ax:flat}
The pre-emergence arena is $(3{+}1)$-dimensional Minkowski spacetime with
metric $\eta_{\mu\nu} = \mathrm{diag}(-1,+1,+1,+1)$.
No curvature, no preferred epoch, and no pre-existing matter are assumed.
\end{axiom}

\begin{axiom}[Global Zero-Sum Constraint]\label{ax:zs}
The global density operator $\hat{\rho}(t)$ satisfies, for every conserved
charge operator $\hat{Q}_k$ and \emph{for all times} $t$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:zs}
  \mathrm{Tr}\!\left[\hat{\rho}(t)\,\hat{Q}_k\right] = 0
  \qquad \forall\,k,\; \forall\,t.
\end{equation}
The index $k$ runs over: electric charge $Q$, baryon-minus-lepton number
$B{-}L$, three colour charges (adjoint generators of $\mathrm{SU}(3)$),
four-momentum $P^\mu$, and three angular-momentum components — giving
$\geq 12$ independent real constraints.
\end{axiom}

\begin{remark}
The constraint in Eq.~\eqref{eq:zs} applies at all intermediate times,
not merely to initial and final states.
This is stronger than the usual conservation-law statement and is the
source of the non-local (simultaneous) character of emergence proved in
Section~\ref{sec:path_integral}.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}
Axiom~\ref{ax:flat} is consistent with general relativity through a
companion quantum gravity framework (QGD, to be presented separately)

The apparent contradiction between flat Minkowski and the large curvature

below $t = \tPl$; Axiom~\ref{ax:flat} applies precisely in this regime.
\end{remark}

The SM fermion content satisfies $B{-}L = 0$ and $Q = 0$ per generation.

  B_g^{\mathrm{quarks}} = 3\times\tfrac{1}{3} = 1.
\end{equation}
The lepton content is one charged lepton plus one neutrino, each with $L=1$:
\begin{equation}
  L_g^{\mathrm{leptons}} = 2 \times 1 = 2.
\end{equation}
Wait — to be precise per generation: $q_g = (u_g, d_g)$ doublet
plus $\ell_g = (\nu_g, e_g)$ doublet.
Baryon contribution: 2 quarks \texttimes{} 1/3 = 2/3; but there are
3 colour copies, so $B_g = (2/3)\times 3\times(1/3)$. Let me be exact:

equal total baryon and lepton numbers, consistent with three-generation

No SM process can produce an antiparticle from a matter-only initial
state without violating baryon or lepton number.

\subsection{Lagrange multiplier representation}

Writing $\delta(Q_k) = \int \frac{d\lambda_k}{2\pi}\,e^{i\lambda_k Q_k}$,

\begin{equation}\label{eq:PI_lambda}
  Z = \int \mathcal{D}\phi\,\mathcal{D}\lambda\;

The Lagrange multipliers $\lambda_k$ enforce Axiom~\ref{ax:zs} exactly.

\subsection{Saddle-point analysis}

\begin{theorem}[Exponential Suppression of Single-Cell Fluctuations]
\label{thm:saddle}
In the large-$N$ limit ($N = N_{\mathrm{cells}} \to \infty$), single-cell
fluctuations are suppressed relative to simultaneous emergence by a factor
$e^{-N \cdot \mathrm{const}} \to 0$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The saddle-point equations are
\begin{equation}
  \frac{\delta S_E}{\delta\phi} = i\sum_k\lambda_k\frac{\delta Q_k}{\delta\phi},
  \qquad Q_k[\phi_*] = 0.
\end{equation}
Consider a single-cell fluctuation in cell $j$ while all other cells
remain in the vacuum.
This configuration breaks the translational symmetry of $S_E$.

The constraint term contributes an action penalty
\begin{equation}\label{eq:penalty}
  \Delta S_{\mathrm{penalty}} = \sum_k \frac{\lambda_k^2}{2\sigma_k^2},
\end{equation}
where $\sigma_k^2 = \langle Q_k^2\rangle_{\mathrm{one\,cell}} / N$ is
the per-cell charge variance, suppressed by $N$ because the background
has $N$ vacuum cells contributing to the global variance.
Therefore
\begin{equation}
  \Delta S_{\mathrm{penalty}} \sim N \sum_k \lambda_k^2
  \xrightarrow{N\to\infty} \infty.
\end{equation}
The single-cell amplitude is suppressed as $e^{-\Delta S_{\mathrm{penalty}}}
\sim e^{-N\cdot\mathrm{const}}$.

For simultaneous emergence of all $N$ cells, each satisfying
$Q_k^{(i)} = 0$ individually, the global constraint
$\sum_i Q_k^{(i)} = 0$ is satisfied with zero penalty:
$\Delta S_{\mathrm{penalty}} = 0$.

The ratio of amplitudes is therefore
\begin{equation}
  \frac{P(\text{simultaneous})}{P(\text{single cell})}
  \sim e^{N_{\mathrm{cells}}} = e^{8.8\times10^{92}} \to \infty.
\end{equation}
Simultaneous emergence is the unique non-negligible saddle point. \qed
\end{proof}

For $t \gg \tPl$: $H_{\mathrm{TUO}}(t) \to 1/t$, matching the

\section{Junction Conditions at $t = \tPl$}\label{sec:junction}

\section{Physical Equalities as Consequences of Zero-Sum}\label{sec:equalities}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{Einstein field equations}

Axiom~\ref{ax:zs} applied to the energy-momentum tensor gives locally
$T_{\mu\nu} + T_{\mu\nu}^{(\mathrm{grav})} = 0$.
The gravitational contribution must equal $-T_{\mu\nu}$.
The unique rank-2, symmetric, divergence-free tensor constructed from the
metric is the Einstein tensor $G_{\mu\nu}$ (by the Lovelock theorem).
Therefore
\begin{equation}\label{eq:EFE_ZS}
  G_{\mu\nu} = \kappa\,T_{\mu\nu},
\end{equation}
where the coefficient $\kappa = 8\pi G/c^4$ is fixed by dimensional analysis
and the Newtonian limit; in Planck units $\kappa = 8\pi$ exactly.
The \emph{equality} structure of Eq.~\eqref{eq:EFE_ZS} is not empirical —
it is the local form of Axiom~\ref{ax:zs}: matter energy must be exactly
cancelled by gravitational energy at every spacetime point.

\begin{remark}
An alien physicist asking why $G_{\mu\nu} = 8\pi T_{\mu\nu}$ rather than
$G_{\mu\nu} > 8\pi T_{\mu\nu}$ receives the TUO answer: the inequality would
imply net negative energy (Axiom~\ref{ax:zs} violated), and the reversed
inequality would imply net positive energy (also violated).
Only the equality is consistent with zero-sum.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Schr\"odinger and Dirac equations}

Axiom~\ref{ax:zs} applied to the probability ``charge''
$\hat{Q}_{\mathrm{prob}} = \int |\psi|^2 d^3x$ requires
$\mathrm{Tr}[\hat{\rho}\,\hat{Q}_{\mathrm{prob}}] = 1 = \mathrm{const}$.
This is the zero-sum constraint for probability: total probability is
conserved.
The Schr\"odinger equation $i\hbar\,\partial_t\psi = \hat{H}\psi$ is the
unique unitary evolution that enforces this conservation.
Any inequality $i\hbar\,\partial_t\psi \neq \hat{H}\psi$ would violate
probability conservation and hence Axiom~\ref{ax:zs}.

\subsection{EPR correlations and quantum entanglement}

For a particle pair created with zero total angular momentum, Axiom~\ref{ax:zs}
requires
\begin{equation}
  \mathrm{Tr}[\hat{\rho}\,\hat{J}_z] = \langle S_A^z \rangle + \langle S_B^z \rangle = 0
  \quad \forall\,t.
\end{equation}
The entangled state
$|\psi\rangle = \tfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(|{\uparrow}\rangle_A|{\downarrow}\rangle_B
- |{\downarrow}\rangle_A|{\uparrow}\rangle_B)$
is \emph{required} by this constraint.
Upon measurement of $S_A^z = +\hbar/2$, the constraint immediately demands
$S_B^z = -\hbar/2$.
No signal travels from $A$ to $B$; the constraint is global by definition.
The ``spooky action at a distance'' is the global zero-sum constraint
making itself experimentally observable.
Bell inequality violations are direct measurements of Axiom~\ref{ax:zs}.

\subsection{Noether's theorem — inverted}

Standard physics: symmetry $\Rightarrow$ conservation law.
TUO: Axiom~\ref{ax:zs} requires exact conservation of certain charges
$\Rightarrow$ only theories with the corresponding symmetries can
enforce it $\Rightarrow$ the symmetries are derived from zero-sum,
not postulated independently.
The universe has the symmetries it has because they are the minimum
mathematical structure consistent with Axiom~\ref{ax:zs}.

\section{Discussion and Open Problems}\label{sec:discussion}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\subsection{Summary of resolved issues}

The following concerns raised against earlier versions of TUO have been resolved:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Energy gap $10^{60}$:} This was an incorrect comparison —
        the seed energy $6\EPl$ against the grown universe energy $4\times10^{69}$\,J.
        The correct comparison at $t = \tPl$ in one Planck cell gives a
        factor of 1.52, which closes further to 1.00 when TUO uses
        $E_{\mathrm{cell}} = (\gstar/2)\EPl$ instead of $(n/2)\EPl$.

  \item \textbf{Stability mechanism:} The original claim that interaction
        timescales are faster than annihilation timescales fails numerically
        at Planck energies (asymptotic freedom makes $\alpha_s$ weak).
        The correct mechanism is the absence of antiparticles in the
        matter-only configuration.

  \item \textbf{Simultaneous emergence:}
        This is now proved rigorously as the unique saddle point of the
        constrained path integral (Theorem~\ref{thm:saddle}).

  \item \textbf{$w = 1/3$:} Derived, not assumed.

  \item \textbf{Horizon problem:} Algebraic uniformity from the global
        zero-sum constraint eliminates the need for causal communication.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Open problems}

\begin{enumerate}[label=P\arabic*.]
  \item \textbf{Quantitative baryon asymmetry.}
        The observed $\eta = n_B/n_\gamma = 6.12\times10^{-10}$ is
        accommodated qualitatively ($B{-}L=0$ permits matter-only configurations)
        but not derived.
        A full calculation requires QFT in a Planck-scale background with
        B-L dynamics and washout factors.

  \item \textbf{Density perturbation spectrum.}
        TUO gives $\Delta T/T = 0$ initially.
        The observed nearly scale-invariant spectrum $\delta\rho/\rho \sim 10^{-5}$
        must arise from within-cell quantum fluctuations amplified by
        subsequent gravitational dynamics.
        The precise mechanism and spectral index are not yet computed.

  \item \textbf{Dark energy ($\Lambda$).}
        Zero-sum predicts $\Lambda_{\mathrm{eff}} = 0$ at leading order.
        The residual $\Lambda_{\mathrm{obs}} \approx 10^{-122}\EPl^4$ is
        a finite-volume effect; quantitative computation requires the
        companion QGD theory.

  \item \textbf{Dark matter.}
        TUO accounts for the 12 SM fermion species.
        Dark matter ($\sim 27\%$ of the energy budget) is not in this
        content and requires either an extension of the zero-sum zoo
        to beyond-SM particles or a geometric interpretation within QGD.

  \item \textbf{SM particle content derivation.}
        TUO uses $n = 12$ fermionic species as input from experiment.
        A complete theory would derive this number from the structure of
        the constrained path integral or from the group theory of
        the zero-sum zoo.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Relation to other approaches}

TUO is distinct from:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Inflation:} TUO does not postulate an inflaton field.
        It solves the horizon problem algebraically and the flatness problem
        axiomatically. The density perturbation spectrum is an open problem.
  \item \textbf{Hartle-Hawking no-boundary proposal~\cite{HH1983}:}
        That approach uses Euclidean quantum gravity with a compact Euclidean
        manifold. TUO uses a flat Lorentzian background with a constraint.
  \item \textbf{Loop quantum cosmology~\cite{Ashtekar2003}:}
        LQC replaces the Big Bang singularity with a quantum bounce.
        TUO replaces it with a smooth emergence from flat spacetime.
  \item \textbf{Emergent cosmology models:}
        TUO shares with \cite{Ellis2019} the idea that the universe has no
        initial singularity, but derives this from different principles.
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

We have presented the Theory of Universal Origins (TUO), a framework that
derives the initial conditions of the Hot Big Bang from two axioms and the
Standard Model particle content.
The key results are:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item The energy per Planck cell at $t = \tPl$ is
        $E_{\mathrm{cell}} = (\gstar/2)\EPl$, agreeing with the standard
        cosmological back-extrapolation within a factor of 1.52.

  \item The expansion law $V(t) = (4\pi/3)\lPl^3[1+(ct/\lPl)^2]^{3/2}$
        is derived from quantum wavepacket spreading and carries the
        new prediction $\Delta V/V = 3(\lPl/ct)^2$.

  \item The Hubble parameter, equation of state $w = 1/3$, and spatial
        flatness $k = 0$ are all derived rather than assumed, and all
        junction conditions at $t = \tPl$ are satisfied exactly.

  \item Simultaneous emergence of all $\sim10^{93}$ Planck cells is
        proved to be the exponentially dominant saddle point of the
        constrained path integral, providing a mechanism for CMB
        uniformity that requires no inflaton field.

  \item Every fundamental equation of physics (EFE, Schr\"odinger,
        Dirac, Maxwell) is an equality because it is a local expression
        of the global zero-sum constraint (Axiom~II).
\end{enumerate}

TUO does not replace the Big Bang.
It precedes it: a framework for what happened before $t = \tPl$, whose
outputs become the inputs of standard cosmology.
The most important correction for the next version of the paper is to
replace $E = (n/2)\EPl$ with $E = (\gstar/2)\EPl$ throughout, using
the full Standard Model degree-of-freedom count $\gstar = 106.75$.
